Verg is an attempt to meet mind to mind with the parts of the paradigm.

How information gets absorbed is constantly changing — shaped by the technology each generation was exposed to. That exposure manufactures culture, creates behavior, builds value systems. And those values drift. Not randomly. They're pulled by the information environment itself.

We're riding a paradigm shift into the threading future of AI technology and the optics of media are shifting underneath us. This is abstract and hard to parse as a human because it's in the ether, in the geist. It takes time and exploration and observation of abstract intangible forms that permeate those who have the mind to project into such preconceived dense dimensions — with imagination and the bandwidth to scope the broad ethereal land being rendered by the absolute unknown.

The internet is about to be flooded with agent-generated content that looks like consensus but isn't. When a thousand blog posts echo the same idea, "trending" becomes meaningless. "Popular" becomes meaningless. The only signal that survives is verified independent convergence — proving that real, unconnected minds arrived at the same conclusion through different paths.

What can be self-evident? What can be stripped down and shown to be true through the data?

Not what's popular. Not what's amplified. Not what most people believe. What independent minds converge on without coordinating — that's the closest thing to truth the internet can produce.

This is a pursuit, not a product. The system measures its own accuracy because I'm honest about not having the answers — only the methodology to search for them.
